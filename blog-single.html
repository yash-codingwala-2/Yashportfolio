Investing in your 20s | How to save for an early retirement?

Published on Nov 26, 2021 by Yash Kumar

Whether you earn 50 thousand or 1 lakh, the money will have no worth if you don't know how to use it. There have been many situations where a man earning lakh per month lost everything because of profligacy. On the other hand, the man who made peanuts successfully earned himself by investing somewhere, which was a significant profit.

Investment is the essential source to get independent, manage your budgets, save for your assets and liabilities and many more. So with this, let's directly dive into those areas where you can invest in your 20s with the few bucks you might earning or getting every month.

1. Savings account

Now, the most basic, widely-known, and risk-free way to invest your money in a savings account. Here you can get 4.00%-6.00% p.a. but it's not to boost your existing money but to keep it safe and get a minimal amount on the total principal you invested.

Millennials tended to grow their wealth in a bank's savings account, but today it's no more like this; many people today tend to invest in other areas rather than saving in a risk-free environment. But being in your early 20s, you should start with the savings account to learn how money interacts, and this is how you save for your early retirement.

2. Post office savings schemes

Another technique of the Millennials, to park their money in the post office savings schemes gives significantly higher interest than the standard savings account. It offers good competition to it.

It would be best if you visited a post office near you, and there you go, you get your money safe and sound. But always bank upon it if it's under govt. Accreditation and will help you a lot to be assured about your wealth. You can open a PPF account near the post office.

3. Stocks

Started shivering? It's a taboo which we have heard from our families, from societies and even from the flix that indulging in the share market will start your downfall, and you will lose every single penny within a short period.

But no, this isn't the case; without proper information and knowledge about stocks, how the market functions and how the market rise and fall, you'll lose your money. You need to study the share market.

For instance - Think your friend asked you for some money, and you gave him cash by analyzing his business model and working strategy, so if he rises, you rise, and if he falls, you fall too. Precisely this is how the stock market functions, and you need to analyze a particular company before investing. And here, you receive your new option to invest and save for yourself.

4. Start your own business

In earlier decades, think about the business, and there you need lakhs to invest. Not anymore, today you need few bucks and a creative mind to start your own business. Many young entrepreneurs began their journey with a minimal 10 to 20k in their hands and an innovative idea.

Their investment in their 20s starts to boom in their 30s, making them multimillionaires. But remember you need a great idea. Think about the CEO and founder of OYO, Ritesh Aggarwal. He started his business with just some 30k with a hotel, and boom, today he is the owner of the largest business chain existing in India.

5. Invest in someone's business

Don't worry! I am not suggesting you again about the share market or to do business yourself. If you don't have time or want your extra amount to be invested somewhere better, I suggest you become a partner with someone in their business. This is how you can expand your network and work efficiency without having all pressure alone on you.

But keep in mind, you need the right amount and sort of investment to invest with this in your 20s because its success depends on how much time you give.

6. Start lending money to people on higher interest

This doesn't seem right ethically, but a great source where you can invest your money to work as an asset. When you lend money to people in need at higher rates, you earn a very good profit on it and if there are any sort of delay, you'll keep having your interest from them, which will be a great source to boost your investment in the 20s.

It's nothing but just a credit card model, as you lend few bucks from the credit card and need to pay with little interest and if there is any delay, then more and more interest. Just keep in mind you need to make money, not destroy someone, because ethics are more important than earning money.

7. Real Estates

If you have earned an outstanding amount and have a property under your name, then congratulations, you have a chance to make more than the initial amount you invested in buying that piece.

Look, I am a big fan of Robert Kiyosaki and his words. He once owned an apartment and later put that building on rent. This is a continuous source for you to have money, but yes, you won't recover your money with the rent only, but it will keep coming till you want. And when you want to sell it, and the market is relatively high, sell it with the proper profit.

The best part about real estate is, physical assets are more worthy than paper assets. Unlike Stocks, where you need to keep information about the national or global market, real estate requires only a local level of knowledge.

Frequently asked questions:

Which Stocks to buy in your 20s?

A - The best way to buy the stock is to learn the market, study it, and analyze the chart pattern. Don't blindly follow the stats being shared on the web and TV. Use your strategy for a better overview.

Is it safe Investing in your 20s?

A - With starting low and keeping your greed in control, and having patience. That's it. Start slow but maintain consistency. You don't even know how much you will have when you grow a seed and keep watering your saving, such as SIP(systematic investment plan).

Which assets to buy in your 20s?

A- Anything that will make more money for you is an asset. Now, look around you and list the things you actually need and generate cash for you.

Any book on how to invest in your 20s?

A - I told you, I am a fan of Robert Kiyosaki so that I will recommend his essential and best selling book " Rich Dad, Poor Dad". Must read from my side.
